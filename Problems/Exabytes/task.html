Exabytes

A bit is the main and the smallest unit of information. Though, we often use other units of information to measure large amounts of data.

The table below shows some units and the correspondence between them:

1 Byte \(2^3\) Bit 8 Bit
1 Kilobyte \(2^{13}\) Bit 8 192‬ Bit
1 Megabyte \(2^{23}\) Bit 8 388 608‬ Bit
1 Gigabyte \(2^{33}\) Bit 8 589 934 592‬ Bit
1 Terabyte \(2^{43}\) Bit 8 796 093 022 208‬ Bit
1 Petabyte \(2^{53}\) Bit 9 007 199 254 740 992 Bit
1 Exabyte \(2^{63}\) Bit 9 223 372 036 854 775 808‬ Bit

Write a program that reads an integer number of exabyte and converts this amount of information into bits.


Sample Input:
1

Sample Output:
9223372036854775808



Memory limit: 256 MB
Time limit: 8 seconds
